Cryptograms

Solve a monoalphabetic substitution cryptogram with a dictionary. Words with the same repeated-letter pattern are candidates; assignments must maintain a one-to-one mapping between cipher and plain letters.

backtrackingpatternsconstraint propagation

Your task

  • Preserve spaces and punctuation.
  • A cipher letter maps to exactly one plain letter.
  • Two cipher letters cannot map to the same plain letter.
  • Return all solutions or an explicit ambiguity/no-solution result.

Example shape

cipher:  IFMMP XPSME
expected shape: HELLO WORLD (for an illustrative shifted example)

Approach ideas

These are strategy summaries, not copied Ruby solutions.

  • Group dictionary words by canonical repetition pattern.
  • Choose the unsolved word with the fewest candidates first.
  • Propagate letter assignments and backtrack immediately on conflicts.
